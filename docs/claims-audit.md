# Claims-safety audit — 13 June 2026

Audit of all user-facing copy against the non-clinical claims guardrail (MHRA
"software is a medical device if it *claims* to diagnose/treat/monitor"; ASA
treat/cure rules; the "wellbeing, not therapy" line in `marketing-strategy.md`
§3). **These changes are staged on a non-deploying branch (`audit/claims-safety`)
— NOT live — because they change how Mandy's AI represents itself and your vision
notes Mandy wants to review the AI.**

## Verdict

The app is **in good shape**. The legal/consent surfaces are genuinely
exemplary. There was **one real problem — and it's the important one:** the
chat **system prompt told the AI it *was* "a highly skilled, evidence-based AI
therapist"** (and, in the fallback, "Mandy Kloppers, a qualified CBT Therapist").
That directly contradicts the app's own Terms, Privacy, and consent gate — and
the entire non-clinical strategy. Fixed (see below), preserving Mandy's voice and
every safety guardrail.

## Already exemplary — no change needed

- **Terms** (`terms.html`): "Not a regulated medical device", "does not create a
  therapist–patient … relationship", replies are "generated by a large language
  model … not medical advice", "supplement to, not a replacement for, clinical
  care". This is the model to keep.
- **Consent gate** (`app.html`): "does not replace clinical therapy, medical
  care, diagnosis, medication, or emergency services" + a checkbox confirming it.
- **About / Privacy:** Mandy's real credentials are correctly attributed to *her*
  (the human therapist), cleanly distinguished from what the app does.
- **Crisis routing** is present and current across the app and the system prompt
  (999/911, NHS 111 opt 2, Samaritans 116 123, SHOUT 85258).

## Fixed (staged on `audit/claims-safety`, awaiting Mandy's sign-off)

**`api/chat.js` (the system prompt — main + fallback):**
- "You are a highly skilled, evidence-based **AI therapist** trained in…" →
  "You are Mandy — a warm, evidence-informed **AI wellbeing companion**. You are
  NOT a therapist and this is NOT therapy or treatment… Your guidance is informed
  by…" (the CBT/ACT/compassion list is kept — *informed by*, not *delivering*).
- Fallback: "You are Mandy Kloppers, **a qualified CBT Therapist** with [creds]…"
  → "the warm voice of Cowch, **built on the approach of** Mandy Kloppers, a
  BABCP-accredited CBT therapist… You are a wellbeing companion, NOT a therapist."
- "that is what good **therapy** does" → "that's what genuinely helps".
- "like a **therapist** who respects your intelligence" → "like a grounded,
  practical guide…".
- **Added** a self-identification guardrail: "If asked what you are, be honest:
  you are an AI wellbeing companion built on Mandy's approach — not a therapist,
  and not therapy. Warmly encourage real therapy or medical help for anything
  clinical."
- **Preserved unchanged:** all crisis handling, the strong anti-diagnostic rules,
  "do not present yourself as a replacement for a human therapist", "do not
  provide medical diagnoses", and Mandy's actual approach/voice/philosophy.

**`manifest.json`:** removed `"medical"` from `categories` (now
`["health","wellness"]`) — it implied a medical product and contradicts the
"not a medical device" posture (your own `store-listing.md` flags this too).

## Flagged — judgment calls for you + Mandy (not changed)

1. **How much should the AI *be* Mandy?** I softened the explicit "you are a
   qualified therapist" self-claim, but the deeper question — how far the AI
   should speak *as* the named, credentialed clinician vs. *in her approach* — is
   Mandy's professional/personal call. Recommendation: keep "her voice and
   approach", avoid "I am a therapist / I am treating you". (The About page
   already commits to "won't pretend she's human" — this just aligns the prompt.)
2. **"Coach" vs "companion".** The manifest name ("AI Wellness Coach") and the
   meta/share/OG copy say "wellness coach" / "built on a real CBT therapist's
   approach". Not violations ("coach" is unregulated), but the strategy voice is
   "companion". A branding-consistency call — left for you since it changes the
   installed app name.
3. **`vision.html` is publicly reachable** (`/vision.html`) and contains the
   internal pricing/funnel deck and framing like "it learns their patterns".
   It's an internal pitch, not product copy — consider `noindex`/unlinking or
   moving it out of `public/`. (Not scanned line-by-line as a product surface.)
