# Cowch — marketing strategy (student beachhead, mission-led soft launch)

_Status: draft for Tom & Mandy, 13 June 2026. Grounded in verified 2024–26

## 0. The strategy in one paragraph

Cowch's wedge is **UK university students**, and its first move is **deepening
one warm relationship — the University of Surrey, on Mandy's doorstep in
Guildford — into a contained, evaluated pilot.** We do *not* try to out-clinical
the incumbents (Spectrum.Life, Wysa, Kooth, Togetherall) or get bought through
heavy NHS procurement. We deliberately sit **outside** the clinical frame: a
**free, on-device, non-clinical wellbeing companion built by a real, named
BABCP therapist**, distributed direct to students (Mandy in the room + her
blog/list), explicitly **not therapy**, that *reinforces* a university's
existing safety pathway instead of replacing it. The same "not therapy, real
therapist, privacy-first" posture that keeps us legally safe is also the trust
story that wins in a moment when generic "AI therapist" bots are being banned and
investigated. Money comes later and gently; we never paywall the student crisis.

## 1. Why now — the opportunity is real and evidenced

UK universities are under acute, documented pressure on student mental health:

- **Disclosed** mental-health conditions rose from **under 1% (2010/11) to 5.8%
  (2022/23)** — but confidential surveys put true prevalence far higher: **~57%
  of students self-report a mental-health issue and ~27% a diagnosed
  condition.** [1]
- Student **suicide incidence rose every year from 2009/10**; the May 2025
  DfE/NCISH **National Review** recorded **107 suspected-suicide deaths and 62
  non-fatal self-harm incidents (169 across 73 providers) in 2023/24**. (Use
  "suspected/reported" framing; never imply Cowch prevents suicide.) [2]
- **70% of those students were already known to university support services** —
  most often wellbeing services. [2]

**What this means for Cowch (the two openings):**

1. **The disclosure gap is the market.** The distance between *disclosed* (5.8%)
   and *true* (~57%) prevalence is the majority of struggling students who never
   come forward. A free, private, on-device companion reaches the people sitting
   **below the institutional radar** — exactly who services can't see.
2. **The gap between touchpoints is the job.** "70% already known" reframes the
   buyer's pain: not *finding* students, but *sustaining* support when human
   services are at capacity or out of hours. Cowch is the **companion in the
   gaps** — never the clinical safety net. (Holding that line is also what keeps
   us out of regulated-device territory — see §3.)

Universities also feel **duty-of-care pressure** even though the law is unsettled
(the _Abrahart v Bristol_ duty-of-care question remains untested; the Equality
Act reasonable-adjustments duty does bind once a disability is known). [1] We can
ride that pressure as a **no-cost, low-risk addition** — never as a mechanism
that "discharges a legal duty" (that claim would be both legally risky and
clinically overreaching).

## 2. Positioning

**The one-liner (internal):** _Cowch is a free, private wellbeing companion built
on a real therapist's voice — here for the everyday, in the gaps the system can't
reach. It is not therapy, and never pretends to be._

**Student-facing line:** _"A calmer space in your pocket — built by a real
therapist, private to you, here whenever you need a moment."_

**What Cowch IS:** a self-directed wellbeing companion; the IMAGINE framework +
tiny daily exercises; a private, on-device journal; one-tap routing to crisis
lines; Mandy's warmth and approach, 24/7.

**What Cowch IS NOT (say this proudly):** not therapy; not a therapist; not a
diagnosis or treatment; not a crisis service; not a replacement for the
counselling, helpline or crisis support a university already provides; not a
data-harvesting app.

**The three differentiators that are actually defensible:**

| Lever | Incumbents | Cowch |
|---|---|---|
| **A real, named voice** | anonymous service desks / generic AI | one trusted BABCP therapist (Mandy), with a public track record |
| **Privacy** | centralised student data | journals on-device; we *can't* see them |
| **Price & posture** | paid institutional contracts, clinical | free, direct-to-student, everyday wellbeing |

## 3. The claims guardrail — the most important section (it protects you)

The line between a **wellbeing product** and a **regulated medical device** is
drawn by **what you claim** (intended purpose) and **how it works** — per the
MHRA's 2025 guidance on digital mental-health technologies. A tool becomes
**Software as a Medical Device** if it claims to **diagnose, prevent, monitor or
treat** a condition (their own red-flag examples: "assesses depression using AI",
"delivers digital CBT for anxiety"). Pure general-wellbeing (mindfulness, mood
tracking, habit-building) stays outside regulation. [3] The **ASA/CAP** rules
reinforce this: claims to "treat/cure/stop/prevent" a condition (or a symptom
like "stops panic attacks") need robust clinical evidence and, for conditions
like depression/OCD/bipolar, professional supervision. [4]

This is also the global direction of travel: **Illinois' WOPR Act (Aug 2025)**
bans AI from providing therapy **and bans advertising "AI therapy"** — but
**explicitly exempts general wellness apps** (meditation, mood trackers). [5]
The **FTC's Sept 2025 inquiry** targets AI **companion** chatbots and their
effects on **minors** (Character.AI, OpenAI, Meta, xAI et al.). [6]

**→ The words Cowch must NEVER use in product or marketing:**
- "AI therapy", "AI therapist", "therapy", "treatment", "clinical"
- "diagnose", "assess", "screen", "monitor your depression/anxiety"
- "treats / cures / stops / prevents / fixes [anxiety/depression/panic/…]"
- "digital CBT for [condition]" (the MHRA's exact red flag)
- anything implying it's a friend/companion to confide in *instead of* people
  (the FTC's parasocial concern), or anything aimed at under-18s

**→ The words Cowch SHOULD use:**
- "wellbeing companion / support / space", "built by a real therapist"
- "CBT- and ACT-**informed** exercises" (approach, framed as self-help, not
  treatment), "reflect", "notice", "a calmer moment", "everyday tools"
- "**not a replacement for therapy**", "if things feel too much, here's where to
  get real help" (and route there)

**Turn the backlash into trust (the narrative):** while generic "AI therapist"
bots are being banned in US states and investigated by the FTC, Cowch is the
**opposite of the thing under scrutiny** — built by a real, accredited therapist,
explicitly not therapy, private by design, 18+, and it actively routes you to
*human* help. That contrast is a marketing asset, not a liability. Keep an
internal **claims checklist** and run every headline/ad/landing line past it.

## 4. Competitive landscape & white space

The UK student/NHS channel is owned by **well-funded institutional B2B players
that compete on clinical credibility**:

- **Spectrum.Life** — "Whole University Approach" tied to the institution's
  *duty of care*; bundles 24/7 counsellor access, structured counselling, remote
  GP. Vendor-claimed 60+ universities. The incumbent to respect, not fight. [7]
- **Wysa / Kooth / Togetherall** — anchor on clinical safeguarding, NHS
  partnership, and human-moderated/clinically-staffed services sold to
  institutions. [8] _(Note: several of Wysa's strongest clinical-evidence claims
  did not survive verification — don't repeat competitor clinical claims as fact;
  it's fair to say incumbents *lean on* clinical-evidence positioning.)_

**The white space is precisely where they aren't:** free (not contracted),
on-device/privacy-first (not centralised), a **single named real-therapist
voice** (not an anonymous service or a generic bot), and **explicitly
non-clinical self-help** (sidestepping the clinical-evidence arms race). Cowch is
**complementary** — the everyday layer beneath an institution's SAP/counselling,
for the 57% who never walk into the Centre for Wellbeing.

## 5. The university beachhead — Surrey first

### Why Surrey is the right first pilot
- **Warm + local:** Mandy is in Guildford and already doing work at Surrey — this
  is deepening a relationship, not a cold pitch. In-person trust is how wellbeing
  tools actually get adopted.
- **Timing hook:** Surrey is a **University Mental Health Charter** programme
  member with a **Charter submission due ~September 2026** (per Surrey's own
  pages) — so its wellbeing team has a live, time-bound need to *evidence*
  concrete actions. A locally-built, free, evaluated pilot is exactly the kind of
  initiative that strengthens that submission.
- **Clean local safety story:** Surrey already runs the **Centre for Wellbeing
  (CWB)**, **Health Assured** (24/7 SAP line + app), **Peer Support**, and points
  students to **Safe Haven Guildford** (crisis drop-in, 6–11pm). Cowch's "Need
  help now?" can route to **Surrey's own** Safe Haven and Health Assured line —
  it *reinforces* their pathway. [Surrey sources below]

### Who says yes, and what they'll ask
- **Owner/buyer:** the **Centre for Wellbeing** (head of wellbeing/student
  support). Secondary allies: **SU welfare officer**, and the **School of
  Psychology** (for evaluation).
- **The Charter is the vocabulary.** Frame Cowch against the "whole-university
  approach" and the **UUK _Stepchange_ domains (Support, Live)** — that's the
  language the buyer evaluates in. (Don't claim "every university has the
  Charter" — only ~100 of ~285 providers do.) [1]
- **The one gate we'll meet — and win on:** if Surrey officially endorses or
  promotes Cowch, that implies a data-processing relationship, triggering a
  **DPIA + supplier information-security questionnaire** (UK GDPR; sector
  standard). [9] **Cowch's on-device, privacy-by-design architecture is the
  decisive advantage here: no central student data → the DPIA/security burden
  collapses.** Lead the institutional pitch with this, and hand them a **one-page
  data-flow/privacy statement** proactively.
- **What we don't trigger:** because Cowch is **non-clinical** and **not bought
  by the NHS/university as a clinical system**, the heavy gates — **DTAC** and the
  mandatory **DCB0129** clinical-risk standard — **don't apply.** [10] That's a
  real speed advantage over the clinical incumbents — *conditional on us holding
  the non-clinical line in §3*.

### Two routes to the pilot (run both)
- **Bottom-up (fast):** Mandy's talks → students install via the share link/QR →
  organic adoption. Needs no institutional sign-off; generates the testimonials
  that make the top-down ask easy.
- **Top-down (durable):** CWB endorsement → light DPIA/security clearance →
  Cowch promoted in Surrey's wellbeing channels → an **evaluated ~50-student
  pilot** (pre/post wellbeing measure, e.g. WEMWBS, run with the Psychology
  school). Gives Surrey Charter evidence and gives Cowch its first evidence base.

## 6. Channel sequencing (not channel soup)

You picked all four channels + the university angle. For a *validate* soft launch
they should fire **in order**, each earning the next:

1. **Warm core — Mandy's blog + email list (Top-10 UK MH blog).** Seed here
   first: announce, get the first cohort, gather reviews/testimonials. Lowest
   cost, highest trust, no cold-audience build needed.
2. **Surrey pilot — the credibility engine.** Runs in parallel; produces the
   proof (student stories + a simple pre/post result) everything else leans on.
3. **Organic social (Mandy / Cowch).** Scale the stories and exercises that land
   (IMAGINE, the cow character, Mandy's voice) — short, human, non-clinical.
4. **Paid ads — LAST, and only to amplify a proven message.** Don't pay to learn;
   pay to scale what already converts. (And keep ad copy inside the §3 guardrail —
   ASA enforces health claims.)
- **Practitioner referrals** run quietly alongside the whole time — a slow,
  compounding layer (therapists/coaches recommending Cowch as between-session
  support).

## 7. The soft-launch plan (the validate stage)

**Goal:** prove the AI quality, the loop, and student value with a small, real
cohort — and come out with testimonials + one evaluation result.

1. **Pre-flight (small, do first):**
   - Sanity-check the §3 guardrail across the live app copy, About page, and
     store/landing text. Fix any "therapy/treat/assess" language.
   - Confirm "Need help now?" lists current UK crisis routes; add **Surrey-local**
     routes (Safe Haven, Health Assured line) for the pilot cohort.
   - Make a **QR code / short link** for Mandy's talks → straight into the app +
     install nudge (the share/OG work already shipped supports this).
   - Stand up the **one-page Surrey pilot pitch** (Appendix A) + the **privacy
     one-pager** for the DPIA.
2. **Seed (warm):** Mandy posts to blog + emails the list — soft, honest, "I
   built this; have a look; tell me what you think." Capture emails + consent.
3. **Surrey cohort (~50):** through a talk / society / CWB channel; light pre/post
   measure; collect quotes and consent to use them.
4. **Listen & iterate:** weekly read of what students actually do and say; fix the
   rough edges; bank testimonials.
5. **Decide:** only after this do we open social + consider paid.

**Measure (validate-stage, not vanity):** installs from the share/QR;
**activation** (first chat / first exercise); **return rate** (came back in
7 days); qualitative student quotes; pilot pre/post wellbeing delta; and the two
events we just wired — `pwa_installed` and `share` (turn Vercel Web Analytics on).

## 8. Monetisation posture — mission-led, deferred

Your call ("no rush; build as we grow; people need this") is the right one and the
research backs it: **free-first, capture emails/consent, build the base.** Two
firm principles:

- **Never paywall the student crisis tier.** Students get Cowch free, full stop.
  A paywall on a duty-of-care product torpedoes both trust and university
  adoption.
- **Monetise *adjacent*, later.** When there's traction, revisit the existing
  tiers (£4.99 Personal / £9.99 Complete) for the *general public* audience from
  Mandy's blog — and the bottom-of-funnel that already exists: **Mandy's own 1:1
  therapy** (Cowch as a warm, ethical top-of-funnel to real sessions). Typical
  consumer-app free→paid conversion is low single digits, so the warm-audience
  funnel + the therapy endpoint matter more than squeezing the free base. (Treat
  exact benchmarks as a later, dedicated piece of work.)

---

## 9. Risks & how we hold the line

- **Claims drift** (the big one): a single "treats anxiety" line could pull Cowch
  into medical-device scope / ASA trouble. → Keep the §3 checklist; review all
  outward copy; never market "AI therapy."
- **Safeguarding expectation:** a wellbeing team will ask what happens in a
  crisis. → Answer clearly: Cowch is *not* the safety net; it **routes to** named
  human services (and to Surrey's own). Don't claim to detect/triage risk.
- **Over-promising the pilot:** keep it small and honest; let the evaluation
  speak.
- **Vendor comparison:** don't disparage incumbents or cite their clinical claims
  as fact; position as **complementary**.
