Explore and strengthen your social connections. These tools help you map your relationships, practice social skills, and navigate difficult conversations with confidence and clarity.

Connection Web

Visualize your relationships as concentric circles. Understanding who's in your inner, middle, and outer circles helps you set appropriate boundaries and invest energy wisely.

Outer Circle
Middle Circle
Inner Circle
Inner Circle: Closest Support People you can be fully vulnerable with. They energize and support you unconditionally. You can call them at 3am.
Middle Circle: Regular Connections Friends, colleagues, and family you see regularly. Mostly positive interactions, though not deep vulnerability.
Outer Circle: Acquaintances Casual contacts, work associates, distant friends. Pleasant but limited emotional investment.

Reflect: Who belongs in each circle? Are there people who drain you that you're treating as inner circle? Are there supportive people you're keeping at arm's length?

Daily Connection Check-in

Combat isolation with small, meaningful gestures. One kind message a day can strengthen bonds and remind you that you're not alone.

Who could you reach out to today?

Text a friend: "Hey, been thinking about you. How are you doing?"
Send appreciation: "I really appreciated when you [specific thing]. Thank you."
Share something small: "Saw this and thought of you [photo/meme/article]"
Check in: "I know things have been hard. Just wanted you to know I'm here."
Make plans: "Want to grab coffee/go for a walk this week?"

Remember: The message doesn't have to be perfect. Reaching out is what matters.

Comfort Zone Ladder

Social anxiety lessens with gradual exposure. Start with small steps and work your way up. Celebrate each rung you climb.

1
Make Eye Contact
Notice people around you. Make brief, friendly eye contact with someone in passing.
2
Smile
Add a small smile when you make eye contact. It's a low-stakes social gesture.
3
Say Hello
Greet someone you see regularly - a neighbor, cashier, coworker. Just "Hey" or "Good morning."
4
Make Small Talk
Add one comment or observation: "Nice weather today" or "Busy morning, huh?"
5
Ask a Question
Ask something simple: "How's your day going?" or "Have you tried [thing]?"
6
Have a Brief Conversation
Let the exchange go beyond one question. Share something about yourself when relevant.
7
Initiate Plans
Suggest a specific activity: "Want to get lunch sometime?" or "There's a thing happening - interested?"

Remember: You don't have to climb the whole ladder today. Pick the step that feels challenging but doable. Practice it until it feels easier, then move up.
