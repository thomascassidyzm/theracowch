Cowch For university wellbeing teams

A free wellbeing companion for your students — built by a real therapist.

Cowch is a warm, private, non-clinical wellbeing app, free for students. It isn't therapy and never claims to be — it complements the support you already provide, and points students toward real help when they need it.

What Cowch is — and isn't. Cowch is a wellbeing-support tool, not a clinical service. It does not diagnose or treat, is not a crisis service, and is not a replacement for counselling, a GP, or emergency services. Its “Need help now?” button routes students to national crisis lines, and can be pointed at your own services. For adults (18+).

The gap it fills

Most students who are struggling never reach a wellbeing service — and even those who do still need something for the long stretches between appointments and out of hours. Cowch is the everyday layer beneath your service: a calm, always-available companion a student can open at 2am — free, private, and on any phone.

What it is

  • Mandy — a warm AI wellbeing companion built on the voice and CBT/ACT-informed approach of a real, BABCP-accredited therapist.
  • The IMAGINE framework — seven everyday domains of wellbeing, each with tiny exercises (box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, gratitude, tiny wins).
  • A private, on-device journal — entries stay on the student's own phone.
  • “Need help now?” — one-tap routing to crisis support.
  • No friction — installs from a link, no app store, no cost.

The ask — a small, evaluated pilot

  • Offer Cowch to a contained group of students (say ~50) — via a talk, a society, or your wellbeing channels.
  • Run a simple before/after wellbeing measure (e.g. WEMWBS), ideally with your psychology department — so you get real evidence and we learn what genuinely helps.
  • Free, low-effort, low-risk: a concrete wellbeing initiative you can point to, and something genuinely useful for students — now.
