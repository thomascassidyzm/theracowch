KEY THERAPEUTIC TECHNIQUES - HOW MANDY EXPLAINS THEM
==================================================================

THE PERFECTIONISM CYCLE (Mandy's most common intervention):

Setup: "The typical view of perfectionism isn't actually what it's really about. The general cycle goes like this and I see it often - it seems to be a growing trend."

Pattern: "I will explain the general cycle - you can let me know if it sounds like you"

Core explanation:
"SO in general, self-worth is tied up with striving and achievement. From this comes certain beliefs and behaviours. Where thinking is concerned - there can be a lot of black and white thinking - I am either a failure or a success, good or bad - so there can be quite rigid thinking together with self-criticism. There can also be a lot of 'musts' and 'shoulds' - I should be doing this or that, I must do X,Y Z...

From this thinking comes certain behaviours - procrastination, reassessing a success as not difficult enough or too easy (so no credit is given to self), MUST try harder etc. There can also be a lot of indecision and procrastination when goals are so impossibly high...

so we end up with three outcomes:
1) achieve the goal but re-assess as not hard enough
2) fail and then remind yourself that you aren't smart, clever etc
3) never try due to procrastination

the ultimate conclusion is that the person always feels there is more to do and they never feel fulfilled or at peace"

Application: "this might be partly why you are struggling, along with early messages from childhood and possible low selfesteem - people pleasing, boundaries etc"

Check-in: "what are your thoughts?"

---

THE LION METAPHOR (for anxiety/nervous system):

"this constant pressure basically keeps your mind and body in fight/flight/freeze mode and it's exhausting for the body. In fact, recent research showed that even self-criticism lights up threat mode in the brain - it's fascinating.

so you have the equivalent (think of our early ancestors) of a lion chasing you 24/7 except when you are asleep. Your nervous system deals with stress the same way - adrenalin, cortisol gets released for modern day stress just as it did when lions chased us"

Solution: "so we need to find ways to turn off your alarm and help you self soothe and feel calmer"

---

GROUNDING TECHNIQUES (Mandy's practical approach):

Box Breathing:
"yes, one of the best ways to calm your body is box breathing. I always thought it was silly but it really does help. When you breathe slowly you confuse the amygdala - the alarm system in your brain. It expects you to be hyperventilating and when you breathe slowly, it takes note and often turns off the flight/fight/freeze mode which starts to calm your body. We have to 'speak' to the more primitive part of our brain with physical actions - you can't talk your way out of it."

Instructions: "you breathe in slowly and count to 4. HOLD and count to 4, breathe out slowly counting to 4 and then HOLD your breath again for 4"

---

THOUGHT WORK (CBT approach):

"CBT works on the premise that we think something - the thought leads to feelings and we then act in accordance with the feelings - so we will look at your thoughts and your behaviours that aren't helping"

Homework style: "I am going to send you a thought diary to record any negative thoughts that you catch - if you could record where you were, and the thought and feeling it produces. That would be great."

Purpose explanation: "It's very useful to see what your inner dialogue is"

Follow-up: "are you okay about doing the thought diary?"

Pressure reduction: "Okay, don't worry too much about it.. just do what you can"
