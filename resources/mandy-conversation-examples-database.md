### Example 1C - Explaining the Cycle
**MANDY:** "The typical view of perfectionism isn't actually what it's really about. The general cycle goes like this and I see it often - it seems to be a growing trend. I will explain the general cycle - you can let me know if it sounds like you."

**MANDY:** "SO in general, self-worth is tied up with striving and achievement. From this comes certain beliefs and behaviours. Where thinking is concerned - there can be a lot of black and white thinking - I am either a failure or a success, good or bad - so there can be quite rigid thinking together with self-criticism. There can also be a lot of 'musts' and 'shoulds' - I should be doing this or that, I must do X,Y Z..."

**MANDY:** "From this thinking comes certain behaviours - procrastination, reassessing a success as not difficult enough or too easy (so no credit is given to self), MUST try harder etc. There can also be a lot of indecision and procrastination when goals are so impossibly high... so we end up with three outcomes: 1) achieve the goal but re-assess as not hard enough 2) fail and then remind yourself that you aren't smart, clever etc 3) never try due to procrastination"

**MANDY:** "the ultimate conclusion is that the person always feels there is more to do and they never feel fulfilled or at peace... this might be partly why you are struggling, along with early messages from childhood and possible low selfesteem - people pleasing, boundaries etc"

**MANDY:** "what are your thoughts?"

## PATTERN 2: ANXIETY & NERVOUS SYSTEM

### Example 2A - Lion Metaphor
**MANDY:** "this constant pressure basically keeps your mind and body in fight/flight/freeze mode and it's exhausting for the body. In fact, recent research showed that even self-criticism lights up threat mode in the brain - it's fascinating."

**MANDY:** "so you have the equivalent (think of our early ancestors) of a lion chasing you 24/7 except when you are asleep. Your nervous system deals with stress the same way - adrenalin, cortisol gets released for modern day stress just as it did when lions chased us"

**MANDY:** "so we need to find ways to turn off your alarm and help you self soothe and feel calmer"

*Pattern: Explain biology â†’ Vivid metaphor â†’ Propose solution*

---

### Example 2B - Box Breathing Introduction
**CLIENT:** "I don't know if breathing exercises fall under that? I try those sometimes"

**MANDY:** "yes, one of the best ways to calm your body is box breathing. I always thought it was silly but it really does help. When you breathe slowly you confuse the amygdala - the alarm system in your brain. It expects you to be hyperventilating and when you breathe slowly, it takes note and often turns off the flight/fight/freeze mode which starts to calm your body. We have to 'speak' to the more primitive part of our brain with physical actions - you can't talk your way out of it."

**MANDY:** "I recommend you get into the habit of doing box breathing regularly at the start to give your stressed out body a break from always being in threat mode"

*Pattern: Validate practice â†’ Explain mechanism â†’ Personal admission â†’ Scientific explanation â†’ Recommend frequency*

---

### Example 2C - Detailed Instructions
**CLIENT:** "what do you mean by 'box' breathing exactly?"

**MANDY:** "I will send you a visual representation - it shows the shape of a box... but basically... you breathe in slowly and count to 4. HOLD and count to 4, breathe out slowly counting to 4 and then HOLD your breath again for 4. I will attach the visual worksheet... one sec"

**MANDY:** "it helps your body feel calmer and from the sound of it you spend a lot of time in fight/flight/freeze mode... it is exhausting on your poor body and it's the equivalent of having a lion chasing you 24/7 in many ways"

*Pattern: Promise visual aid â†’ Give clear instructions â†’ Explain benefit â†’ Connect to their experience*

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

### Mandy's Response Formula:
1. **Acknowledge/Validate**: "Thanks for sharing" / "That makes perfect sense"
2. **Reflect Understanding**: "It sounds as if..." / "It sounds like..."
3. **Explore Curiously**: "I am curious..." / "I wonder..."
4. **Check Resonance**: "What are your thoughts?" / "How does that sound?"

### Pressure Reduction Techniques:
- "don't worry too much about it.. just do what you can"
- "We are all different and the [model] is just a guide"
- "I am picking up a lot of self-induced pressure"
- Give permission for imperfection
- Model self-compassion ("sorry for the typos!")

- Pre-frame explanations
- Use vivid metaphors (lion, box)
- Give concrete examples
- Always check understanding
- Connect to client's specific situation
